Leather

leather is made from the chemically strengthened and softened hide of an animal (such as a cow, sheep, pig, or goat). Leather is frequently used to make shoes, backpacks, coats, handbags, and other accessories because it is durable and waterproof. Suede is a soft but not water-resistant form of leather created from the underside of the animal hide. There is also vegan leather, or synthetic leather, which is frequently constructed of PVC. Polyvinyl chloride, a type of plastic, is used to create costumes and flashy, waterproof garments. PVC is frequently used to make vegan leather.
